## Project Executive Summary

This repository documents my Digital Literacy Portfolio, completed as part of
the CSE0001 course at VIT Bhopal University. As a first-year student, I have
worked through five structured modules designed to build a professional,
secure, and well-rounded digital presence. The project spans visual
communication, professional networking, technical platforms, email etiquette,
and cybercrime awareness — equipping me with the foundational skills expected
of a modern engineering graduate.

---

## Detailed Module Reflections

### Module 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
**Objective:** To create a visual resource that communicates digital literacy
concepts clearly to fellow students.

**Execution:** Using Canva, I designed a one-page infographic covering what
digital literacy means, safe internet practices, and the importance of a
professional online presence.

**Key Learning:** The most challenging part was deciding which information to
prioritise — keeping the design clean while ensuring the most important points
remained visible and easy to understand.

---

### Module 2: Student Digital Portfolio
**Objective:** To establish a professional digital identity across key platforms.

**Execution:** I set up and connected my presence on GitHub, LinkedIn, and
Kaggle. My GitHub profile README introduces my branch, year, and learning
goals, while my LinkedIn outlines my academic background at VIT Bhopal.

**Key Learning:** I understood that a digital portfolio is not a one-time task
but a living document — one that grows alongside my skills and becomes a
verifiable record for future internship and placement opportunities.

---

### Module 3: Coding & Collaboration Platforms
**Objective:** To build hands-on familiarity with tools used in technical
education and teamwork.

**Execution:** I completed a beginner-level challenge on HackerRank to
practise algorithmic thinking. I also built a 5-question Digital Literacy
Awareness Quiz using Google Forms, linked to a response spreadsheet for
tracking batchmate responses.

**Key Learning:** Building a real quiz for peers — rather than just completing
an exercise — made the task feel purposeful. Cloud-based tools like Google
Workspace are genuinely useful for academic collaboration.

---

### Module 4: Professional Email & Etiquette Guide
**Objective:** To develop professional written communication skills for
academic and industry contexts.

**Execution:** I drafted two formal emails — one requesting a deadline
extension from a professor, and one expressing internship interest to a company
coordinator. I also created a 10-point Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist.

**Key Learning:** Professionalism in communication is not about being overly
formal — it is about being clear, concise, and respectful of the reader's time,
with a clear purpose in every message.

---

### Module 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention
**Objective:** To understand and communicate real digital threats faced by
students in India.

**Execution:** I wrote a detailed case study on Phishing, covering how attacks
are carried out, who is targeted, and what the consequences are. I also
developed an 8-point prevention checklist with specific UPI safety tips and
references to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and
Helpline 1930.

**Key Learning:** What surprised me most was how heavily phishing relies on
creating a false sense of urgency. I have personally committed to verifying any
"urgent" link against the sender's official domain before clicking.
